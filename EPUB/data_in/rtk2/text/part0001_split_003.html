The above group of kanji with their on readings represents by far the easiest treated in this book. Still, it is important to master them well before moving on, if for no other reason than to make sure you have understood what signal primitives are and how they work.

When you are reviewing (see Introduction), do so only from the compound (not the character) to the reading and meaning—not the reverse. And remember not to clog your memory with useless information (for example, which signal primitives share the same pronunciation). Once you are confident of your progress so far, you can go on to the next group of 114 kanji. They are also of the “pure” type, but number only 3 to a group.
