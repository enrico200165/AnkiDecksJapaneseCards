chapter 9

A Potpourri of Readings

The main thing the kanji of this chapter have in common is that they do not fit the previous categories and are a bit too common to leave for the final chapter. It is arguable that a few of the compounds might have been included in the last

chapter, and some left for the next. But there is no getting around the fact that we have come to the point where we must enlist the full assistance of brute memory.

To make the leftovers a little less formidable, I have cooked up a potpourri of exemplary compounds that I hope you find interesting or instructive enough to hold your attention while you are learning them. In some cases this has meant choosing an unusual compound; the readings, in any event, are commonly used.

The ages of 25, 42, and 62 for men and 19, 33, and 37 for women have traditionally been regarded as bad-luck years in Japan. Though not all the numbers are remembered by people today, no one is likely to forget 42, and no woman 33, especially as those years draw close. (Note that the second character is read with its kun-yomi.)
